Economic Authorship Framework Gains National Attention
By Andrea Brvenik, Founder

The movement began with a simple premise: that every person has the right and the capacity to design their own economic future. What started as a framework inside IntexiaU Corp has grown into a national conversation about workforce identity and self-determination.

A New Vocabulary for Work

Economic Authorship offers a new language for an old problem — how do individuals navigate a labor market that was never designed with them in mind? The framework's four pillars — Clarity, Strategy, Authorship, and Momentum — give practitioners a repeatable path.

"You cannot author a life you haven't first imagined in full."

Brvenik, who recently declined a CEO offer to stay focused on IntexiaU, says the decision was itself an act of Economic Authorship. "I had to practice what I preach," she notes.

AI Reshapes Credentialing as Employers Shift Focus
Staff Report

Employers across sectors are quietly abandoning degree requirements in favor of demonstrated competency — a shift that AI-native platforms like IntexiaU are uniquely positioned to serve.

The trend, accelerating since 2023, has significant implications for traditional universities, online program managers, and the millions of Americans who hold credentials that no longer open the doors they once did.

What It Means for Workforce

IntexiaU's Platform of Everything — a ten-module hub connecting education, credentialing, and employment — is designed specifically for this inflection point.

Podcast Transcript Excerpt

In this week's episode, investor and tech pioneer Rick Inatome sits down with Andrea Brvenik to discuss how he thinks about capital allocation, the Series A process, and why legacy matters more than liquidity events.
